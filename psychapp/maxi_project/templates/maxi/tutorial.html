Syllogisms

An argument is a group of statements about the state of the world. If valid, the components of some statements, called premises, are arranged in such a manner so that is is impossible for the them to be true and another statement, called the conclusion, not be true. The premises are said to "imply" the conclusion.

A syllogism is an argument that has two premises and one conclusion A syllogism is categorical when all statements
